"Du Pont Co. reported that third-quarter profit grew a robust 19% from a year ago on the strength of the company's operations in various chemicals and fibers, and in petroleum.
Event 1: Du Pont reported a 19% increase in profit from a year ago.
Event 2: Du Pont raised its quarterly dividend to $1.20 a share from $1.05, which will increase the annualized payout to shareholders by some $140 million.
Event 3: Du Pont's stock rose $2.50 a share to close at $117.375 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday.
Event 4: Du Pont's industrial products segment profit soared to $155 million from $99 million a year earlier.
Event 5: Du Pont's petroleum segment profit grew to $98 million from $71 million a year earlier.
Event 6: Du Pont's diversified businesses segment profit grew to $64 million from $27 million.
Event 7: Du Pont's fibers segment profit rose to $180 million from $155 million.
Event 8: Du Pont's coal segment profit fell to $41 million from $58 million.
Event 9: Du Pont's polymers segment profit dropped to $107 million from $122 million.
Event 10: Du Pont earned $2 billion, or $8.46 a share, up 18% from $1.69 billion, or $7.03 a share, a year earlier.
Event 11: Du Pont's sales increased 10% to $26.54 billion from $24.05 billion.
Event 12: Du Pont announced plans for a 3-for-1 stock split, although the initial higher dividend will be paid on pre-split shares.
Event 13: Du Pont's chairman and chief executive officer, Edgar Woolard Jr., stated that the company intends to increase rewards to shareholders.
Event 14: Du Pont's largest beneficiary will be Seagram Co., which owns about 23% of Du Pont.
Event 15: Du Pont's stock rose $2.50 a share to close at $117.375 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday."
